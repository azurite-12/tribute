Marty Friedman

One of the greatest metal guitarrists of all time

Here's a chronology of Marty's career, achievements and feats.

  • 1962 - Marty is born in Washington D.C, United States of America.
  • 1976 - At age 14, he started playing guitar after attending a KISS concert, which inspired him.
  • 1981 - He forms the band Hawaii, which recorded a few demos and an EP, but disbanded in 1986.
  • 1986 - Along with his friend, Jason Becker, her formed Cacophony, an instrumental metal band, which released 2 albums until it disbanded in 1989.
  • 1988 - Marty starts his solo career and releases Dragon's Kiss, a great record for instrumental metal fans.
  • 1990 - Marty joins Megadeth, and releases Rust in Peace, the band's very best record, and one of the most influential metal albums of all time.
  • 1992 - Despite joining Megadeth, Marty continues with his solo career and releases Scenes, Introduction and True Obsessions during his time at the band. Drummer Nick Menza collaborated with him during this period.
  • 2000 - After growing frustrated with the way Megadeth was going with its artistic direction, he leaves the band and decides to focus on his solo career.
  • 2002 - Two years after his departure from Megadeth, Marty releases Music for Speeding, a succesful return to his solo career.
  • 2003 - Marty moves from America to Tokyo, Japan. And proceeds to become a household name in the J-Rock scene.
  • Present day - Marty remains active and proceeds to collaborate with many rock and idol groups in Japan, making their songs become hits and become very popular in the country.
